It's all about $65-$67 depending on how much 3d printer filament we end up using. However, we already have some of the stuff. However, because we researched more, we found out that we didn't even need bluetooth, we could just use infrared to send and read signals. This was all possible due to IRremote, a library for Arduino that reads infrared signals and decodes them. This is possible because the remote we were going to use comes with an IR receiver. However, we did need to get a mini-breadboard for it. On Wednesday and Friday, we were designing our projects in TinkerCAD. Jonah and I had slightly different designs, but both could still work.
