Semester 1 Final

Weeks 14-15

Spaghetti Bridges

The project: Build 4 prototypes and 1 final bridge with only 100 sticks of spaghetti, with maximum 20 sticks per bridge. Wood glue can be used to hold the bridge together.
The bridges will be scored by the wieght they hold vs. how much they weigh (in-class competition score, not grade score).
My group: Jonah Barty, Theodore Perry, and Taylor Larson.
Bridge 1: "The Line" - 6 columns of 3 sticks of spaghetti put together end-to-end in a line. Held a full bucket, was the best design.
Bridge 2: "The Rod" - 3 attatched parts of 6 sticks spun into a wire. Wood glue was literally poured on it, therefor it's full bucket strength isn't as impressive as the line.
Bridge 3: 6 strips of 3 spaghetti connected side-by-side. 1 of them was used to make 2 X’s at the end, connected in the middle by the other one. Random in-between parts to take the tension. Pretty meh.
Bridge 4: Similar to bridge 3, but has an X in the middle as well. The X’s at the edge cross closer to the end. Like bridge 3, pretty ok.
Bridge 5 (Final): a lot like bridge 3, but with more rungs. Unfortunately, some unfortunate happenings fell on the bridge and it was missing an end and a rung. It weighed 16.3g, held 687g of water, and had an overall ratio of 42.1. We got 5th place if you don’t consider the cheater rod.
