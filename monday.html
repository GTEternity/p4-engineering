Mechanical Engineering

Definition:

an engineering branch that combines engineering physics and
mathematics principles with materials science to design, analyze,
manufacture, and maintain mechanical systems. It is one of the
oldest and broadest of the engineering branches.

Don't worry, we were taught the proper safety procedures, but we haven't used them yet.

Mechanical engineering opens up into:
CAD, manufacturing, thermodynamics, material science,
fluid mechanics, and structural analysis.

There are also simple machines, which are basic mechanisms that do basic tasks.
These are:
Wheels and axles: used to move things in something like a cart.
Levers: Move something up when something weighs upon the other side.
Pulleys: Use one thing going down to move something on the other side up.
Inclined Planes: Ramps to move things down (or sometimes up).
Wedges: Put something in between 2 other things to move them apart.
Screws: Can hold things together or convert rotating force into linear force.
